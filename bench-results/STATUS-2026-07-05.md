## Current standing vs rg (bench_vs_rg, 2026-07-05)

- `--paths-only`: at or near parity with `rg -l`. On /usr/src/linux several cases
  are FASTER than rg (0.86-0.99x). Synthetic corpus ~1.1-1.3x.
- Match mode (human grouped output): 1.15-2.9x slower than `rg -n`. Profile shows
  the gap is dominated by structure extraction + matched-file re-read + rg
  subprocess fork/exec, NOT the lexical scan.
- Geometric mean ratio over all cases: 1.39x.

## Next steps (designed, not yet implemented)

The profiling wave produced a full design for a native in-process engine
(drop the rg subprocess entirely): parallel `ignore::WalkBuilder::build_parallel`
walk, `memchr::memmem` fixed-string scan, `grep-regex`/`grep-searcher` for regex,
retain matched file text for the structure phase (zero re-reads), early-exit for
paths-only. Expected wins per the profile: ~0.4-0.8ms fork/exec + parse overhead
on small repos, 5-27ms re-read + structure cost on dense queries.

Key artifacts to consult: the profile baseline doc above and the swarm audit
findings (dense-query structure extraction is the dominant cost, threshold
constants in src/search.rs control the dense-skip behavior).
